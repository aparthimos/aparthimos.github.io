Concordia SAE

Concordia SAE is a student-run chapter of SAE International, A world-renowned engineering organization, at Concordia University. Concordia SAE is comprised of three different teams, FormulaE, Baja, as well as aerodesign, that compete at SAE international events.These yearly international competitions allow students to apply classroom knowledge to real-life situations, challenging students to design, manufacture, test, and compete with a vehicle in a dynamic environment. I am incredibly proud to be the President of Concordia SAE and to be able to focus on the improvement of SAE as well as its members. For the last two years I have worked with members, students and staff to advance Concordia SAE to a new level of competition by incorporating SAE as a non-profit and gaining independence, working to increase funds, doubling SAE's membership and improving community outreach.

SAE Baja Racing

As a member of Baja Racing I have spent hours designing, manufacturing, assembling and disassembling the vehicle. Through this I have become proficient in the use of software like Solidworks, Fusion and Ansys needed to design, manufacture and improve the vehicle. I have also developed a deep understanding of the drive-train, steering, electronics and suspension subsystems.

Steering System Redesign

A full ground-up redesign eliminating welded assemblies that introduced warping and alignment issues. Machined interfaces replace all welded joints for precise geometry control.

  • Complete elimination of welding — removes all warp-related alignment errors
  • 2-pound overall weight reduction through topology-optimised geometry
  • Reduced part count and manufacturing complexity, lowering assembly time
  • ANSYS Static Structural FEA validation at peak cornering loads

2-Speed Helical Gearbox

A 2-speed helical gearbox where gear geometry is optimised by an ML model trained on pinion performance data. Combines classical gear design theory with a data-driven approach.

  • Helical geometry selected for reduced noise and higher load capacity over spur gears
  • Python ML model trained to predict tooth root bending stress and contact fatigue life
  • Automated loop iterates pressure angle, helix angle, and module for minimum mass
  • Full assembly modelled in SolidWorks with housing designed for FDM prototyping
